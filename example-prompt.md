**SCENE GENERATION PROMPT TEMPLATE**

Write a vivid, fully realized scene based on the details below. The scene should feel cinematic and immersive, with a strong balance between narration and dialogue.

**Core Instructions:**

* Use descriptive narration to establish mood, setting, and character actions.
* Use dialogue frequently, but make it purposeful and natural (avoid filler).
* Keep the pacing dynamic: alternate between action, internal thought, and spoken lines.
* Show emotions through behavior, tone, and subtext—not just explicit statements.
* Avoid overly long monologues unless dramatically justified.
* Let the scene *breathe* with small details (gestures, pauses, environment).

---

**INPUT DETAILS**

* **Setting:** [Where and when the scene takes place]
* **Characters:** [Names + brief traits or motivations]
* **Context:** [What just happened before this scene]
* **Goal of Scene:** [What needs to happen or be revealed]
* **Tone:** [e.g., tense, romantic, eerie, chaotic]
* **Conflict:** [Internal or external tension driving the scene]

---

**STYLE GUIDELINES**

* Write in **third person (or specify first person if desired)**.
* Use **tight, sensory descriptions** (sight, sound, touch).
* Keep dialogue formatted clearly:

  * New line for each speaker
  * Minimal dialogue tags; use action beats instead

---

**OUTPUT FORMAT**

Write the scene as a continuous passage, blending narration and dialogue smoothly.

---

**OPTIONAL ENHANCEMENTS**

* Include subtext (characters not saying exactly what they mean)
* Add micro-tension (interruptions, hesitations, shifting power dynamics)
* End with a hook, shift, or unresolved beat

---

**EXAMPLE ADD-ON (Optional):**
“Make the dialogue sharp and slightly indirect, with characters avoiding saying what they truly feel.”
